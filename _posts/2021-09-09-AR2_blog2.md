<h1 id="what-is-already-there">What is Already There</h1>
<p>The core problem is that to model a high-dimensional joint density distribution requires exponentially many parameters as the dimension going up. The following methods are different approaches to circumvent this issue by making assumptions, simplifications, or viewing it from another perspective.</p>

<h2 id="source-of-randomness">Source of Randomness</h2>
<p>We know that, specfically for VAEs, there is a reparameterization trick that separates the source of randomness to a standard Gaussian distribution. Now we models the quantile function, how do we get samples from it? Where is the source of randomness now?</p>
<p>It is <span class="katex--inline"><span class="katex"><span class="katex-mathml"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>τ</mi><mo>∼</mo><mi mathvariant="script">U</mi><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mo stretchy="false">[</mo><mn>0</mn><mo separator="true">,</mo><mn>1</mn><mo stretchy="false">]</mo><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\tau\sim\mathcal{U}([0,1])</annotation></semantics></math></span><span class="katex-html" aria-hidden="true"><span class="base"><span class="strut" style="height: 0.43056em; vertical-align: 0em;"></span><span class="mord mathnormal" style="margin-right: 0.1132em;">τ</span><span class="mspace" style="margin-right: 0.277778em;"></span><span class="mrel">∼</span><span class="mspace" style="margin-right: 0.277778em;"></span></span><span class="base"><span class="strut" style="height: 1em; vertical-align: -0.25em;"></span><span class="mord mathcal" style="margin-right: 0.09931em;">U</span><span class="mopen">([</span><span class="mord">0</span><span class="mpunct">,</span><span class="mspace" style="margin-right: 0.166667em;"></span><span class="mord">1</span><span class="mclose">])</span></span></span></span></span>. Since quantile functions are essentially inverse CDFs, taking uniform random <span class="katex--inline"><span class="katex"><span class="katex-mathml"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>τ</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\tau</annotation></semantics></math></span><span class="katex-html" aria-hidden="true"><span class="base"><span class="strut" style="height: 0.43056em; vertical-align: 0em;"></span><span class="mord mathnormal" style="margin-right: 0.1132em;">τ</span></span></span></span></span>, and feeds it to the model will give us a sample back. <p>Authors recognized that most current state-of-the-art models is built on top of development of Autoregressive modesl, VAEs, and GANs, which they all employ KL-divergence as the measure between two distributions. Now, we use the quantile function and quantile loss to achieve the same tasks, which may be more suitable for certain tasks that cares for low density region of the distrbution.</p>
<p>Although this new approach will not reduce training/inference time, it signifies an important perspective to look at the density estimation.</p>
