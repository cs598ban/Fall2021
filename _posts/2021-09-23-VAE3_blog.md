<h2 id="introduction">Introduction</h2>
<p>This blogpost talks about deep hierarchical VAEs - why do we need deep (instead of shallow) hierarchy, what are the main challenges, and how do to design them.</p>
<p>It is commonly known that VAE variants generates much less realistic images than GANs. One major hypothesis is that the latent space sturcure is too limited: there is no hierarchy and we try to fit a simple prior (e.g. a standard Gaussian) to it. When we want to increase its capacity while keeping the divergence loss tractable, the idea of <strong>hierarchy</strong> naturally comes to mind.</p>
<p>Ladder VAE (LVAE) is an early example of hierarchical VAE. In addition to dividing the latent space into multiple layers, it also enables bidirectional (<em>bottom up</em> and <em>top-down</em>) flow of information from layer to layer. The models we will discuss below are based heavily on its design.</p>
<p> <img src="{{ site.baseurl }}/images/vae3blog1/lvae.png" alt="Architecture of very deep hierarchical VAE." title="Inference and generative processes of LVAE."> </p>

<p>The latter structure alone doesn’t bridge the gap bbetween GANs and VAEs, but we will see below that models based on its design will finally reach a performance comparable to SOTA generative models.</p>
<h2 id="nvae">NVAE</h2>
<p>Nouveau VAE (NVAE) focuses on improving performance through architecture refinement. The hierarchical architecture is based on LVAE, but with difference scale per layer of latent variables.</p>
<p> <img src="{{ site.baseurl }}/images/vae3blog1/nvae.png" alt="VAE model" title="Inference and generative processes of NVAE."> </p>
<p>There are four main improvements:</p>
<h3 id="residual-cells-in-encoder-and-decoder">Residual Cells in Encoder and Decoder</h3>
<p>Adding residual connection to VAE is easy, but to realize long-range correlation in data is tricky. One straightforward way is to increase the kernel size of convolution layers in the decoder, but then we face the problem of large parameter sizes. To solve this problem, NVAE uses depthwise separable convolution from MobileNetV2 <sup class="footnote-ref"><a href="#fn1" id="fnref1">1</a></sup>.</p>

<strong>Proposition 2.</strong> <em>N-layer VAEs are universal approximators of N-dimensional latent densities.</em></p>
